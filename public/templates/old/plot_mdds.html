Melt Degree Days
Melt Degree Days (MDDs) are a measure of sensible heat flux on the glacier. They are calculated the same way as Cooling Degree Days [1], which are often used in heating and energy use calculations. MDDs can be accumulated over the melt season by adding up the previous days' MDDs. Because cumulative MDDs have a time-series component and the Bering Glacier's ablation rate is fairly steady, cumulative MDDs and ablation correlate very well. Melt Degree Days DM for a day with maximum Tmax and minimum Tmin temperatures; base temperature Tbase is melting point (0Â°C).

When Tmax < Tbase, DM = 0
When (Tmax + Tmin)/2 < Tbase, DM = (Tmax - Tbase)/4
When Tmin <= Tbase, DM = (Tmax - Tbase)/2 - (Tbase - Tmin)/4
When Tmin > Tbase, DM = (Tmax + Tmin)/2 - Tbase

[1] From Vilnis Vesma, Degree Days Direct
